Track, intensity, and wind-radius data: IBTrACS v04r01 (NOAA NCEI), Western Pacific basin. Wind circle radii (Beaufort level 7/10/12 — JTWC's 34/50/64kt, per quadrant) are JTWC (USA agency) reports, which is why coverage starts in 2001 — systematic quadrant reporting didn't exist before then, for any storm in this archive, so it can't be extended back to 1985. Radius shapes interpolate between the four reported quadrant extents; treat them as an approximation, not a precise wind-field boundary. The Dvorak T-number is estimated from each point's wind speed via the standard Dvorak CI-number/wind-speed table — a standard approximation, not an independent satellite reading.
